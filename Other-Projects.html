Miscellaneous Projects

Below is a collection of different projects ffrom across different disciplines. I really enjoy learning about different topics and that often results in creating a varierty of projects. These projects are combination of skills I've learned in school, work, and on my own.

Spot Micro Quadruped Robot (WIP)

Spot Mini is an opensource quadrupedal robot based off of Boston Dynamic's Spot robot dog. The spot micro use 12 servo motor to actuate the limbs and is controlled via an Arduino Mega and a bluetooth reciever.

This is currently a work in progress but the model has been 3D printed and assembled as well as wired together. The Spot Micro has two ultrasonic sensors as well as a LCD display for sensing and calibration. The service routines to actuate each of the limb motors has been created.

The Spot Micro now needs to be trained using OpenAI gym and interfaced with the arduino code to actuate the limbs. In addition, an adroid app will be created for remote control.

FarmBot Construction

FarmBot is an open source CNC sytle farming robot that allows for automous farming. During my time at Notre Dame High School, I lead a small team to construct, program, and integrate two FarmBots within the Notre Dame greenhouse.

I created a woodenplatform based on given constraints and fixed aluminum extrusions for the robot to traverse on. Once the robotics were assembled, I created different farming sequences to pick up tools, plant and water crops, and monitor their growth on a daily basis!

Discord Streaming Bot

Discord is group messaging platform that allows for users to create servers with numerous channels (chats) with many different users. I created a discord bot using the Discord API in Python that took text chat prompts from the Discord chat to search YouTube and play live music in a voice channel.

The Discord bot ran natevly on my computer and would perform a YouTube webscrape for a requested song and download the .MP3 file. The bot would then join the voice channel and play the requested song. The bot supported pausing/playing music, queuing songs, and creating custom playlists which were stored in permenant .txt files.

Bridge Construction Competition

As part of the MTE 219 (Mechanics of Deformable Solids) course at the University of Waterloo, the final design project was to design, optimize, and laser cut a truss bridge from balsa wood. Different designs were created, tested and refined with a final design being able to support ovcer x300 times it's weight.

AutoCAD was used to design members and geometries were optimized based on common failure methods (buckling, rupture, tensile, etc.)

Woodworking River Tables

River tables are made from live edge slabs of wood that are joined together using an epoxy resin to form a 'river'. The top table on the right is made from poplar and the bottom from oak. I cut both trees down myself using a chainsaw and planed them down using a planar.

Working with epoxy resin is a unique learning experience as you have to heat it correctly and ensure the wood is sealed correctly, otherwise you will end up with bubbles suspended in it! I also created the legs by MIG welding some 1/4" square tubing together.

Woodworking - Maps 

I have made a couple of wooden topographical maps for different fantasy worlds! The top map is the entire Game of Thrones map (Westeros and Essos) with the major houses labeled. The bottom map is of Tamriel from the Elder Scrolls Series.

Both of these were based on reference images I could find online and had a ton of fun making them. I used a scrolls saw to cut each of the individual landforms out and then shaped them with a dremel. 

I was thinking of making one of the world soon as well!
