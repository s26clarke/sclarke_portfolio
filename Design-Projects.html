Project Summaries

Power Hammer System

A power hammer is used in industrial forgining applications to forge hot steel and metals. I modeled, simulated, optimized, and ultimately constructed a physical power hammer prototype for personal forging use. 1-D simulation data was used to select appropriate motor specifications, identify methods of failure and enhance the design.

Skills: CATIA, Mechanism Design, Dymola, Functional & Logical Design, Motor Circuitry & Control Logic, Data Analysis & Optimization

Model Nasa Starshade Satellite

The NASA Starshade is a deployable 'origami' satellite that aims to shield a space telescope from the light of surrounding stars to allow for superior photography of exoplanets. A Hoberman frame was developed to study the kinematics of the starshade expansion according to NASA specifications as well as FEA models were prototyped to model the thin-folding of solar "pedals" and an internal mesh.

Skills: CATIA, Mechanism Design, Surface Modeling, Abaqus CAE, Functional & Logical Design

GO TO PROJECT

Mechanical Power Hammer System

Project Overview

The goal of the mechanical power hammer system was to design a fully functional model using CATIA and Dymola using realistic and data and then fabricating it for personal use.

What is a Power Hammer?

Power Hammers are used in industrial forging environments to form heated steel to rough shape.

A ram is driven via a mechanical or pnumatic/hydraulic force to deform the material (typically a motor).

It is vital to be able to measure and control the amount of force being applied by the hammer.

Design Objectives & Approach

CAD Model

  1. Create a to-scale CATIA model for the power hammer and each of the subsystems
  2. Join each of the subsystems together in a single flexible model
  3. Construct mechanisms with a signle controlled DOF to actuate the main assembly to model the motor, the step bar control, and the driving of the ram

Dymola & FLE Simulation

  1. Create Dymola systems models for the motor circuitry and step bar control
  2. Generate Dymola Kinematic models for each of the CATIA mechanisms
  3. Combine Dymola models and CATIA kinematics together in a single logical framework in FLE Designer environment
  4. Run simulation test cases with different motors to ensure a viable prototype
  5. Determine the force output of the hammer at different times

Physical Prototype Construction

  1. Create the rocker mechanism and base for the hammer
  2. Weld a mounting bracket together and fix a drive shaft
  3. Attach the motor to the main assembly
  4. Ensure the assembly can support a full range of motion

1. CATIA Model

A power hammer consists of 7 distinct sub assemblies:

  • A Base - Houses all the components
  • A Motor - Drives the mechanical assembly
  • A Ram  - Oscillates up and down and deforms the metal
  • A Drive Shaft  - Spins according to the motor
  • A Crank Wheel  - Turns rotational into translational motion
  • A Rocker  - Transfers motion from the crank to the ram
  • Step Pedal  - Controls the speed of the motor

Based upon historical models, the dimensions for the base was approximated and the remaining parts were created to be proportional to the base. For the initial design, geometries were typically made bulkier to ensure a viable model.

Mechanism Design

Controlled rotational angular mechanisms were created for the main drive, the motor, and the step bar within the main assembly with only a  single degree of freedom (DOF) without any commands.

This was achieved through carefully selecting the required engineering connections to include in the assembly and resepective mechanisms.

It's important that the mechanisms were created this way to ensure an accurate simulation.

2. Logical Architecture Design

FLE Architecture

The FLE logical architecture frame work has logical components that are joined using logical conenctions to exchange data. Each component is nested within a parent component and contains CATIA models and Dymola behaviours.

Main Drive

The Main Drive Component contains the Dymola models for the main assembly and motor kinematics as well as the motor circuitry. 

The motor circuitry actuates the mechanical components in the main drive.

Step Bar Control

The Step Bar Control component actuates the mechanical step bar in the assembly as well as sends a control signal to the motor circuitry in the Main Drive component based off of a pre-defined routine.
